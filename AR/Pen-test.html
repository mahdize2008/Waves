Penetration Test

The use of computer networks, in addition to the facilities it provides to organizations, it also exposes them to problems and threats.

Such problems and threats may put the networks at risk when the networks do not have proper and safe design and management. Such networks unknowingly open the doors for hackers and malware.

When networks are hacked or when they are infected with malware, irreparable losses are brought to the network. Hence, creating security through proper design and management of a network is among the highest priorities.

In order to evaluate and find existing security weaknesses, various methods of penetration testing are used. In these tests, using hacking techniques, an attack is simulated to determine the security level of a network or system. This helps an organization to have a realistic assessment of its network security in terms of detection and the ability to respond and make appropriate decisions in time. The result of this test is a report that is used for network implementation and technical inspections.

Penetration testing methodology

Black Box Test:

In this method, penetration testing experts perform the test without any information from the network.

White Box Test:

In this method, penetration testing experts perform a test with complete information about the existing network and infrastructure.

Gray Box or Crystal Box Test

In this method, penetration testing experts, like an employee of the organization, have the right to access resources that other employees can also access, and with this type of access, they provide testing.

Future Waves usually uses the third method to perform penetration testing, in such a way that the employer provides two nodes from its network and public access to the penetration testing experts.
Then the following tests are performed:

  • Browsing and visiting the network
  • Browsing the status of ports
  • Software specifications of the systems
  • Promotion in the field of services
  • Network testing against vulnerabilities
  • Use of existing exploits and systems resistance
  • Application testing
  • Penetration tests to obtain passwords
  • Testing denial of service attacks
  • Security assessment of wireless networks
  • Review of remote accesses
  • Penetration testing of routers and communication equipment
  • Penetration testing of security equipment and software including: Firewall, IDS/IDP/IPS and....
  • Database penetration test
