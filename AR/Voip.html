VOIP

VOIP stands for Voice over Internet Protocol. This term refers to the transmission of voice, fax and related services through IP packets and specifically the Internet. This technology compresses telephone voice data and transmits it along the signal data lines that are used today for Internet and intranet communication.

VoIP significantly reduces the hardware and physical space required to support the communication technology. In every internet and intranet network a large amount of bandwidth is not used. The technology uses this idle bandwidth and uses it to transmit voice data along with digital data (instead of using PSTN lines).

Advantages of VoIP

1. Cost

The cost of implementing IP-Telephony is much lower than the implementation of a normal telephone system. The most important reason is that this technology uses a network for voice and data transmission.

For example, the cost of the phone for long-distance calls is greatly reduced. It can be said that the cost of implementing an IP-Telephony system for a company with 450 users will save 20-30% compared to the implementation of an old telephone system.

Undoubtedly, the most important advantage that can be considered for VoIP systems is the cost reduction. For example, if the connection between two places equipped with VoIP system is established through a point-to-point link, the total communication costs between these two offices will be eliminated, or in the case of international communication, these costs will be reduced to a very significant level. Also, since the numbers are assigned to IP phones, if the user's location changes, the costs of moving or transferring the phone number will disappear.



2. Support Maintenance

Since the IP-Telephony system uses the same technology that exists for data, it will create many benefits for employees. In some companies, the management and utilization of the system increases up to 30%. Also, the cost of maintenance and support is significantly reduced.



3. Transfer, addition and changes

IP-Telephony system is easy to use after implementation. You will be able to have a variety of services. For example, wherever you travel in the world, you can take your phone with you and there is no need to change your phone number, and the cost is much lower as well. Some communications companies such as Skype provide services that only use the internet to make and receive phone calls. Also, VoIP is a digital system that provides many services along with Voice at very good prices, such as Call Redirection, Video Conversation, Audio Conference, Call Recording, CDR Reporting, Call Queuing, etc.



4. Countless possibilities

Due to the fact that information is exchanged digitally in the VoIP system, many features can be achieved by using software facilities. For example, the possibility of taking CDR from the established calls, limiting the call to a specific destination for everyone or for a group of users or even for one person, facilities such as Call Waiting, Call Forward, Call Transfer, Call Parking, Call Pickup, Ring Group, Follow Me, etc. .

Also, the following possibilities can be realized by VoIP: sending calls to voicemail, playing voice messages, adding address books via phone or web, increasing or decreasing the required user facilities via web, making video calls according to the type of IP Phone, no restrictions to increase the number of users, sending and receiving faxes through the above system etc. .
