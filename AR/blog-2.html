HRM principles **guide employees towards safe behavior**

Human risk management (HRM) enables businesses and organizations to assess, monitor and mitigate the ongoing security posture of their employees against cyber threats and human error. As these threats become more complex, HRM provides a comprehensive solution to guide safe human behavior, rather than relying solely on user training.

There are four key elements to human risk management:

  • 1. Guide the user towards safe behavior

    Security awareness training is one of the most effective approaches to reducing human cyber risk, with businesses expecting to see an ROI of between 69 and 562 percent, according to statistics. These sessions, which are provided through computer-based training, should be regular and short and cover various information security and related topics.

  • 2. Reduce vulnerability to phishing

    Phishing simulations not only allow businesses and organizations to assess human vulnerability to common attacks, but also provide an opportunity to enhance the measurement of each user's progress and, if necessary, retrain the user. Ideally, simulations should be performed quarterly to test new and updated attacks while assessing the risk level of new employees.

  • 3. Improve security processes

    Implementation of the policy management process helps employees to understand their responsibilities and act based on them in order to strengthen the protection of the organization's information and assets. Policies should address a number of key areas (examples are listed below) and should be updated annually and signed off by staff.

  • 4. Reducing external threats

    With millions of usernames, passwords, and payment details placed on the dark web every year, continuous monitoring of the dark web alerts businesses that employee data is at risk. has fallen.
    Identifying these early threats can ultimately prevent a targeted attack and potential data breach in the future.

9 tips to deal with long-term human risks:

  • 1. Make the training short and interesting

    Use short video training courses to train employees.

  • 2. Cover the essentials

    Be sure to cover key security topics (some are listed below).

  • 3. Train employees regularly

    Monthly training updates the security awareness of employees and makes them more alert against cyber threats.

  • 4. Avoid technical terms

    Many employees do not understand technical terms.

  • 5. Repeat common phishing threats

    Test employees against scams they are likely to encounter.

  • 6. Implement quarterly phishing simulations

    This helps monitor and mitigate risk.

  • 7. Main policy coverage

    Make sure your policy covers the essentials (some are listed below).

  • 8. Keep policies up to date

    Review and update policies annually.

  • 9. Measuring impact

    Evaluate the performance of training and simulations over time.

Some key training topics for your staff

  • Phishing attacks
  • Password and authentication
  • Safe work from home
  • Safe use of the Internet and email
  • Physical security
  • Social engineering
  • Mobile device security
  • Public Wi-Fi

Some common phishing scams to test on your workforce

  • Unread notification messages in the judicial system
  • Text messages related to the peer-to-peer system (mobile phone registry plan) with the aim of abusing bank information
  • A phone call from WhatsApp messenger claiming to win with the intention of misusing banking information
  • New requests (via email, etc.) from technical or management teams
  • Safety information about viruses like Corona
  • Office 365 password expiration
  • Disable old OneDrive account
  • New voice message alert

An example of an essential security policy to implement in your business or organization

  • Confidential Data Policy
  • Email Policy
  • Mobile device policy
  • Network security policy
  • Password policy
  • Physical security policy
  • Incident response policy
  • Business continuity policy
